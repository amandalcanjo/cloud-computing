Benefits and Disadvantages

Cloud Computing Benefits

With technological advances and the search for information security, companies are forced to keep up with these advances to remain competitive in the increasingly fierce market.

We have listed below a number of benefits that cloud computing can provide to the user.

Economy/low cost

Costs vary according to the defined service model, and can eliminate everything from data storage to the IT infrastructure completely without loss of quality in data access and information management.

Collaboration

Cloud solutions allow more than one employee to work simultaneously in a secure and stable way. Collaboration tools allow joint editing and video conferencing meetings.

Availability

The provider will always try to eliminate points of failure and create redundancy. If one server fails there will be another one to replace it instantly.

Scalability

It is possible to hire only the necessary resources, being able to expand or reduce these resources according to the demand, eliminating equipment with lack of resources or excess.

Safety

IT teams are often overwhelmed and do not have enough time to devote to security aspects and users' greatest concern is with regard to stored data. Backups performed on the web platform will be protected in case of local disasters.

Management

Cloud computing delivers capabilities through the resource center and infrastructure managed by contracts backed by the SLA. IT infrastructure updates and maintenance are eliminated as all resources are maintained by the service provider.

Strategic use

Cloud computing allows you to forget about technology and focus on your business activities and helping with the optimization of the latest applications thus increasing your productivity.

Cloud Computing Disadvantages

Despite the many benefits, entrepreneurs and IT professionals also need to pay attention to the disadvantages that are provided by these services.

Downtime

With internet connection failure and technical failures, the services provided by providers may be temporarily suspended once the application is running in the cloud.

Security

The use of cloud services indicates that providers will have access to important company information. While these providers use high security standards and certifications, the facilities to access cloud services can also attract malicious users and provide the ability to exploit loopholes. Example of multi-tenant cloud architectures where the server is shared with others.

Supplier lock

Even though cloud services are touted as a flexible and easy-to-migrate environment. The application developed by a certain platform may not run on another when migrated from the environment.

Limited Control

The customer will be allowed to control only operated applications, data and services. The control of the infrastructure, updates and firmware are in the hands of the service provider.
