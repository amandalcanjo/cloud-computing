Types of Services

Cloud architecture is due to three categories of services provided, each with its particularity and specification. It can even completely replace the physical structure inside the establishments. Below we will comment on the types of services offered by cloud providers:

Software as a Service (Saas)

This is the basic model, marketed in the form of a service. The customer does not need to worry about the physical infrastructure of the equipment as this part will be under the responsibility of the supplier, as well as the maintenance and in some cases the backup of the applications. This model is very pleasing to interested parties, as the customer pays for the contracted resources and can upgrade the resources according to their demand. The end user will be able to access the information anytime and anywhere using an internet browser of their choice and a link provided by the supplier.

We can use as examples known sites such as: Facebook and LinkedIn as well as Google Docs, Gmail and Conta Azul applications.

Platform as a Service (PaaS)

This model delivers a platform for developing applications that will be provided in the SaaS model. The physical structure of the equipment as well as the SaaS will be under the responsibility of the supplier and the resources will be upgraded according to demand. The purpose of this type of service is to solve security problems in accessing databases, interfaces and APIs, in addition to removing application integration limitations. The application development takes place within the platform using available libraries and functions. The disadvantage is the dependence on the supplier, depending on the evolutions and the risk of the project taking different paths from the one intended by the contracting party.

Examples of providers: Elastic Beanstalk, Azure WebApps, Google App Engine.

Infrastruture as a Service (IaaS)

In this model, the customer rents the physical equipment in a datacenter and in return receives all the infrastructure and hardware. This type of service is marketed through server virtualization. The Goal is to replace the entire on-premises IT infrastructure for an outsourced cloud service. The customer does not need to worry about drivers and operating system, availability of electricity and access links as well as the network and routing part will be managed by the Datacenter.

Examples of IaaS: Amazon EC2, Azure Virtual Machines, and Google Computing Engine.

After knowing the services offered and being able to better assess which service model will be best for the company, professionals will be able to identify which type of cloud will be used.
