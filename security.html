Cloud Computing Security

After defining the entire structure of the cloud, before migrating the information, we need to verify the security it will provide. With each new emerging technology, new threats and challenges are also born, the issue of security in Cloud Computing, focuses on the most critical area when it comes to data storage in the cloud: data confidentiality.

The cloud brings an additional level of risk, directly associated with outsourcing essential services (cloud services). This requires special attention to the observance of information security principles: availability, integrity, confidentiality and authenticity, as well as privacy, support and compliance.
To ensure the confidentiality of a customer's data, cloud computing providers enforce strict privacy policies employing proven encryption methods to authenticate users in their environments. An even safer measure would be to encrypt the data before storing it on a cloud provider.

Despite all the security provided by data centers, information security professionals must pay attention to the principles of data security as listed below:

User privileges

Determine access permissions and monitor the network through logs with credentials, allowing you to identify any anomalies. The administrator privilege should only be assigned to the area leader and the misuse of this privilege allows for security breaches.

Regulatory Compliance

Part of data security and integrity are the responsibility of the contracting company. It is necessary that a regulation is created, charged and complied with by the IT professional so that hard-to-resolve loopholes do not arise.

Data location

The IT manager must charge the cloud provider for the commitment to store and process data under specific legislation â€” preferably in the country where your company is based.

Data segregation

It is common for a company to share an environment with data from several customers. Therefore, it is important to research how the provider isolates data within its structure and the type of encryption used.

Data recovery

The cloud service provider must ensure with the customer the location of data and backup routines, as well as a data recovery plan and estimated time for this task to be performed.

Research support

It is important that the contracted cloud provider has, by contract, a commitment to follow the legal definitions of the country in which the structure is maintained and used, in addition to pre-defined roles and processes to act in front of an audit.

Long-term viability

Even if the provider is a large and consolidated company in the market, it is still subject to problems and changes in the economy that make its delivery difficult or make its service less attractive to your company as a cloud customer. Therefore, the contract between the two parties must ensure that this stored data will be available for retrieval and transfer in case you want a migration.

Audit process

SAS 70: auditing standard developed by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA). The services focus of this auditing standard is on companies that provide data processing, payroll generation, accounting and other specific business processes.

SSAE 16: attestation standard issued as a certification standard aimed at resolving commitments by service auditors.

ISAE 3402: provides an audit process for the processes performed in the provision of services to clients, in addition to international standardization options to ensure the delivery of reports with two fundamental objectives: obtaining an acceptable level of security, using the most relevant aspects and criteria appropriate, and create reports with the conclusions found by the auditor.

NBC TO 3402 (Brazilian standard based on ISAE 3402).

Defined norms and standards:

ISO 27001 Certification: standard prepared to provide a model capable of establishing, implementing, operating, monitoring, critically analyzing, maintaining and improving an Information Security Management System (SGSI).

PCI DSS: Payment Card Industry (PCI) Data Security Standard (DSS) designed to encourage and enhance data security and facilitate the widespread adoption of consistent data security measures around the world.

Technologies and services:

SysTrust: A type of warranty service jointly developed by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) and the Canadian Institute of Chartered Accountants (CICA).

Identity and Access Management (IAM): Access and Identity Management is an information technology related to processes for managing who has access to the information system in question.

Multi-factor Authentication (MFA): security system characterized by the presence of more than one form of authentication, implemented to verify the legitimacy of a transaction.

Token: technology based on authentication based on devices belonging to the user. It is hardware commonly used as an additional authentication mechanism providing higher level security.

Cryptography: science that underlies the various technologies and protocols. It's the science of keeping messages safe.
